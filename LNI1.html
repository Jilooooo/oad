Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Define the term negotiable instrument and explain its meaning.
  • Identify the characteristics of negotiable instruments.
  • Explain the functions and importance of negotiable instruments.
  • Describe the life cycle of a negotiable instrument from issuance to discharge.
  • Recognize the essential requisites of a negotiable instrument.

Definition of Negotiable Instrument

The word negotiable means transferable by delivery, and the word instrument means a written document by which a right is created in favor of a person.

Thus, a negotiable instrument is a written document that creates a right in favor of someone and is freely transferable by delivery or by endorsement and delivery.

It entitles a person to a certain sum of money and can be transferred from one person to another.

Characteristics of Negotiable Instruments

1. Free Transferability

They are freely transferable without formalities. Ownership passes by endorsement or delivery.

2. Holder‚Äôs Title is Free from Defects

A bona fide holder for value gets good title free from previous defects.

3. Consideration

Every negotiable instrument is presumed to be issued for consideration.

4. Date

They usually bear a date which indicates maturity or validity periods.

5. Transfer Before Maturity

Negotiable instruments are generally transferred before they mature.

Functions of Negotiable Instruments

  • Serve as a substitute for money.
  • Increase purchasing medium in circulation.
  • Have definite value without needing further verification.
  • Serve as equivalent to money.

Incidents in the Life of a Negotiable Instrument

1. Issuance

The first delivery of the completed instrument to a holder.

2. Discharge

  • Payment in due course by the principal debtor.
  • Payment by the party accommodated.
  • Intentional cancellation by the holder.
  • Any act that discharges a simple contract.
  • When the principal debtor becomes the holder at maturity (merger).

Requisites of a Negotiable Instrument (WUPPI)

  • Must be in writing and signed by the maker or drawer.
  • Must contain an unconditional promise or order to pay a sum certain in money.
  • Must be payable on demand or at a fixed/determinable future time.
  • Must be payable to order or bearer.
  • Drawee must be named or indicated with certainty.
