Learning Objectives

What is a Contract of Sale?

A contract of sale is an agreement where one party (seller) transfers ownership of a thing to another (buyer) for a price certain in money or its equivalent.

  • The seller delivers the item.
  • The buyer pays the price.
  • Ownership transfers upon delivery, unless agreed otherwise.

Essential Elements of a Sale

  • Consent ‚Äì freely given agreement of both parties.
  • Object ‚Äì the item/goods being sold; must be lawful and possible.
  • Cause ‚Äì the price in money or its equivalent.

Sale vs. Other Contracts

  • Sale ‚Äì exchange of goods for a price (money).
  • Barter ‚Äì exchange of goods for goods.
  • Donation ‚Äì transfer of ownership without payment.
  • Lease ‚Äì temporary use of property for rent.

Rights and Obligations of the Seller and Buyer

  • Seller must:
    • Deliver the item sold.
    • Ensure the item has no hidden defects.
    • Transfer ownership peacefully.
  • Buyer must:
    • Pay the price at the agreed time and place.
    • Accept delivery of the goods.
    • Check goods upon delivery.

When is Ownership Transferred?

Ownership passes from seller to buyer upon delivery of the item, unless the parties agree on another mode.

Importance of the Law on Sales in Business

  • Ensures fair trade and protects both parties.
  • Provides remedies when goods are defective or payment is not made.
  • Clarifies rights and obligations to avoid disputes.
  • Supports smooth commercial transactions.
