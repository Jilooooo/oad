What Are Sources of Law?

Sources of Law refer to the origins from which legal rules, rights, and obligations are derived.

  • They guide how laws are created, interpreted, and enforced.
  • They provide the legal basis for resolving disputes and regulating conduct.

Primary Sources of Law

  • Constitution ‚Äì The highest law of the land; all laws must conform to it.
  • Statutes (Legislation) ‚Äì Laws passed by Congress.
  • Administrative Regulations ‚Äì Rules issued by government agencies.
  • Judicial Decisions ‚Äì Supreme Court interpretations (doctrine of stare decisis).
  • Local Ordinances ‚Äì Laws enacted by cities, municipalities, or barangays.

Secondary Sources of Law

  • Legal Commentaries ‚Äì Books and writings by legal experts.
  • Law Journals ‚Äì Research articles and case analyses.
  • Legal Dictionaries ‚Äì Definitions of legal terms.
  • Textbooks ‚Äì Academic explanations of legal concepts.

Why Are Sources of Law Important?

  • They guide courts, businesses, and individuals in understanding legal rules.
  • They ensure laws are properly interpreted and consistently applied.
  • They provide a reliable basis for business decisions and compliance.
  • They help avoid conflicts and legal disputes.
