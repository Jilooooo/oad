Introduction of Events Management

Events Management is a function requiring public assembly for the purpose of celebration, education, marketing, and reunion. It is a process that includes:

  • Research
  • Design
  • Planning
  • Coordinating
  • Evaluation of events

Events

  • An occurrence; something that happens.
  • Unique happenings that bring people together for a purpose.

Special Events

It is a one-time or infrequently occurring activity outside the normal programs or activities of the organizing body.

Characteristics of Events

  • They are unique occurrences that do not happen very often.
  • They have a limited duration (happening within hours or days).
  • They occur with a live audience present.
  • They require one or more groups to plan and organize.
  • They are staged for a purpose.

Types of Events According to Purpose

A. Celebration

Events that bring people together to celebrate milestones, accomplishments, rituals, and ceremonies.

Examples: Life cycle events, religious rituals, commemorative ceremonies, socio-civic events

B. Commune

Events that strengthen friendships and restore relationships.

Examples: Reunions, hallmark events, sporting events

C. Convene

Events that gather people to share and pass on knowledge.

Examples: Meetings, conferences, conventions

D. Commerce

Events that promote and market product offerings.

Examples: Marketing events, exhibits, trade shows

Types of Events According to Size

A. Mega Events

Largest events, targeted at international markets.

Example: Olympic Games

B. Hallmark Events

Events with distinctive qualities that promote tourism destinations.

Examples: Sinulog Festival (Cebu), Ati-Atihan (Kalibo)

C. Major Events

Large-scale events with strong public interest and media coverage.

Example: Super Bowl

Types of Events According to Nature

  • Entertainment Events
  • Social / Lifecycle Events
  • Corporate Events
  • Sport Events
  • Religious Events
  • Educational Events
  • Political Events
  • Charitable / Fundraising Events
  • Raising

Summary

Events Management plays an important role in society by creating meaningful experiences, promoting culture, strengthening communities, and supporting the economy. Events may vary according to purpose, size, and nature but all require careful planning, coordination, and evaluation. By understanding events and their significance, individuals and organizations can deliver successful and impactful gatherings aligned with sustainable development goals.
