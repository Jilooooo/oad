Lesson 2 – Kinds and Classifications of Legal Forms

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Explain the various kinds and classifications of legal forms based on purpose, formality, and content.
  • Identify examples of legal forms within major classifications.
  • Distinguish contractual, declaratory, authorization, property/financial, and government forms.
  • Describe differences among simple written, notarized, and registered forms.
  • Understand unilateral, bilateral/multilateral, and conditional legal forms.
  • Select appropriate legal forms for different real-life scenarios.

Introduction

Legal forms come in various kinds and classifications, each designed for specific purposes and legal functions. Understanding these classifications helps users choose and prepare the correct document for any legal or business transaction.

Classification Based on Purpose

A. Contractual Legal Forms

Establish binding agreements between parties.

  • Employment Contract
  • Lease Agreement
  • Sales Contract
  • Service Agreement

B. Declaratory Legal Forms

Declare facts or intentions; often sworn under oath.

  • Affidavit of Loss
  • Affidavit of Support
  • Declaration of Heirship

C. Authorizations and Powers

Grant authority to another person to act legally on one’s behalf.

  • Special Power of Attorney
  • General Power of Attorney
  • Authorization Letter

D. Property and Financial Instruments

  • Deed of Sale
  • Lease Contract
  • Mortgage Agreement
  • Promissory Note

E. Government and Institutional Forms

  • Application Forms
  • Certificates
  • Permits and Licenses

Classification Based on Formality and Execution

A. Simple Written Forms

Require signatures but no notarization.

Examples: Receipts, Simple Agreements

B. Notarized Forms

Require a notary public to authenticate signatures.

Examples: Affidavits, Deeds of Sale

C. Registered Forms

Must be registered with government agencies to be effective.

Examples: Land Titles, Mortgages

Classification Based on Content

A. Unilateral Forms

Bind only one party.

Example: Affidavit

B. Bilateral or Multilateral Forms

Bind two or more parties to obligations.

Example: Contract of Sale

C. Conditional Forms

Effectiveness depends on a condition.

Example: Conditional Sale Agreement

Practical Examples and Applications

TYPE DESCRIPTION EXAMPLE
Contractual Legal Forms Binding agreements with obligations Employment Contract
Declaratory Legal Forms Sworn statements attesting to facts Affidavit of Loss
Authorization Forms Grant legal authority to another person Special Power of Attorney
Property and Financial Forms Transfer or encumbrance of assets/debts Deed of Sale
Government/Institutional Official forms for compliance Business Permit Application
