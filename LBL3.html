What is an Obligation?

An obligation is a legal tie that binds a person (debtor) to perform a duty in favor of another (creditor).

  • The debtor is required to give, to do, or not to do something.
  • The creditor has the right to demand fulfillment of the obligation.

Essential Elements of an Obligation

  • Active Subject (Creditor) ‚Äì the party entitled to demand performance.
  • Passive Subject (Debtor) ‚Äì the party who must perform the obligation.
  • Object / Prestation ‚Äì the act or thing to be given, done, or not done.
  • Juridical Tie ‚Äì the legal bond that binds both parties.

What is a Contract?

A contract is a meeting of minds between two persons where one binds himself to give something or render a service to another.

  • Contracts create obligations enforceable by law.
  • They are essential in business for regulating agreements and ensuring fairness.

Requisites of a Valid Contract

  • Consent ‚Äì Must be freely given by parties with legal capacity.
  • Object ‚Äì The subject matter must be lawful, possible, and determinate.
  • Cause ‚Äì The reason or purpose for entering into the contract.

Importance of Obligations & Contracts in Business

  • Ensure predictable and enforceable business transactions.
  • Protect parties from fraud, breach, or misunderstanding.
  • Provide legal remedies when agreements are not fulfilled.
  • Establish trust and professionalism in commerce.
