What is Labor Law?

Labor Law refers to the set of rules and regulations that govern the relationship between employers and employees. It ensures fairness, safety, and protection in the workplace.

  • Protects the rights of workers.
  • Ensures employers follow legal standards.
  • Promotes harmonious and productive work environments.

Basic Rights of Employees

All employees are entitled to fundamental rights, including:

  • Right to Just Compensation ‚Äì receiving proper wages and benefits.
  • Right to Security of Tenure ‚Äì employees cannot be dismissed without valid cause.
  • Right to Safe Working Conditions ‚Äì protected under Occupational Safety and Health Standards.
  • Right to Self-Organization ‚Äì forming unions or employee groups.
  • Right to Rest Days and Leaves ‚Äì regulated under labor standards.

Employer Obligations

  • Provide safe and healthy working conditions.
  • Pay wages on time and in accordance with minimum wage laws.
  • Follow rules on working hours, overtime, and rest periods.
  • Give legally mandated benefits (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, 13th month pay).
  • Avoid unjust or illegal dismissal of employees.

Types of Employment

  • Regular Employment ‚Äì work necessary to business operations; provides full rights and benefits.
  • Probationary ‚Äì trial period (up to 6 months); assessment of employee performance.
  • Project-Based ‚Äì hired for a specific project; employment ends when the project ends.
  • Seasonal ‚Äì work dependent on seasons (e.g., harvesting).
  • Casual ‚Äì work not usually necessary to the business.

Labor Standards vs. Labor Relations

  • Labor Standards ‚Äì refer to minimum employment conditions such as wage, hours of work, and benefits.
  • Labor Relations ‚Äì governs interactions between employers and workers regarding unions, collective bargaining, and dispute settlement.

Importance of Labor Law

  • Protects employees from exploitation.
  • Ensures employers follow fair and legal employment practices.
  • Helps reduce workplace disputes.
  • Promotes safe, just, and productive working environments.
