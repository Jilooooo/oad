Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of conclusions and recommendations in a business report.
  • Write clear and actionable conclusions based on findings.
  • Formulate practical and evidence-based recommendations.
  • Apply professional and ethical standards in report writing.
  • Demonstrate accuracy, integrity, and objectivity in reporting.

Introduction

A business report is not complete without strong conclusions and recommendations. These sections provide clear summaries of important findings and suggest specific actions that decision-makers can take. In this lesson, you will learn how to write effective conclusions, develop recommendations, and uphold ethical standards in professional reporting.

Writing Conclusions

Purpose: To summarize key findings from the report.

  • Base conclusions on facts, evidence, and analysis‚Äînot opinions.
  • Avoid introducing new information or unrelated ideas.
  • Be clear, concise, and direct to highlight the main message.
  • Explain what the findings mean for the business or organization.

Writing Recommendations

Purpose: To suggest actions based on findings and conclusions.

  • Be specific, realistic, and aligned with the report‚Äôs findings.
  • Back up each recommendation with evidence or data from the report.
  • Use action-oriented words such as implement, improve, develop, monitor, or strengthen.
  • Explain how the recommendation will benefit the organization.

Professional and Ethical Reporting

  • Accuracy ‚Äì All facts, figures, and information must be correct.
  • Objectivity ‚Äì Present data honestly and avoid personal bias.
  • Confidentiality ‚Äì Protect sensitive and private business information.
  • Clarity ‚Äì Information must be easy to understand.
  • Responsibility ‚Äì The writer must take ownership of the report‚Äôs content and quality.

Importance of Conclusions and Recommendations

  • Helps managers understand the results quickly and accurately.
  • Guides decision-making, planning, and future actions.
  • Shows the writer‚Äôs analytical and critical thinking skills.
  • Promotes ethical, responsible, and credible business communication.
