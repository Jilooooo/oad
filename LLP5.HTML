Lesson 5 – Court Procedures and Case Management

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the structure of the Philippine Court System.
  • Identify the stages of a civil case.
  • Recognize the duties of a legal secretary in court-related tasks.
  • Learn how courts function and how cases progress.
  • Explain basic trial processes and documentation requirements.

Structure of the Philippine Court System

A. Supreme Court

  • Highest court in the Philippines.
  • Composed of the Chief Justice + 14 Associate Justices.
  • Final authority on all legal cases.

B. Court of Appeals (CA)

  • Second highest court.
  • Reviews decisions of RTCs.

C. Sandiganbayan

Court handling graft and corruption cases involving public officials.

D. Court of Tax Appeals (CTA)

Handles tax disputes involving the BIR or BOC.

E. Regional Trial Courts (RTCs)

  • Trial courts of general jurisdiction.
  • Handle major civil and criminal cases.

F. MTC / MeTC / MCTC

  • Handle minor civil and criminal cases.
  • Small claims and violations with penalties under 6 years.

Stages of a Civil Case

1. Filing of Complaint

Plaintiff submits a complaint to initiate the case.

2. Issuance of Summons

Defendant is formally notified of the case.

3. Filing of Answer

Defendant responds to the allegations.

4. Pre-Trial Conference

Meeting to define issues, mark evidence, and attempt settlement.

5. Trial Proper

Presentation of witnesses and evidence.

6. Judgment or Decision

The judge issues the final ruling.

7. Execution of Judgment

Court enforces the decision in favor of the winning party.

Duties of a Legal Secretary in Court-Related Tasks

A. Filing Pleadings

  • Prepare and submit pleadings (Complaint, Answer, Motions).
  • Ensure documents are complete, signed, and duly notarized.
  • File on time and secure receiving copies.

B. Coordinating with Court Clerks

  • Verify case numbers, schedules, and orders.
  • Secure certified copies if necessary.

C. Scheduling Hearings

  • Maintain and update a court calendar.
  • Remind lawyers of deadlines and hearing dates.
  • Inform clients of updates and progress.

D. Record-Keeping

  • Organize documents by case number or client name.
  • Maintain copies of pleadings, evidence, receipts, and court orders.

Summary

Court procedures ensure fairness and order in resolving legal disputes. Legal secretaries play an essential role in filing pleadings, scheduling hearings, coordinating with court staff, and managing case records. Understanding the court system and case flow allows legal office personnel to perform their duties efficiently and professionally.
