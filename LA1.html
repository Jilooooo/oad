What is Accounting?

According to the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (1961), ‚ÄúAccounting is the art of recording, classifying and summarizing in a significant manner and in terms of money, transactions and events which are, in part at least, of a financial character, and interpreting the results thereof.‚Äù

It is known as the language of business because it communicates the financial condition and performance of an organization.

Purposes of Accounting

  • Record all business transactions accurately.
  • Determine profit or loss during a specific period.
  • Show the financial position of the business.
  • Provide data for planning, budgeting, and decision-making.
  • Comply with government and tax requirements.
  • Protect the business against fraud and errors through internal control.

Importance of Accounting

  • Helps monitor income and expenses.
  • Builds trust and transparency among investors, lenders, and stakeholders.
  • Supports accurate reporting to agencies such as BIR and SEC.
  • Keeps financial records organized for decision-making and growth.
