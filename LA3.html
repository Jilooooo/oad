What is DBA?

The Double-Entry Bookkeeping System (DBA) is a method of recording financial transactions where every transaction has two effects ‚Äî a debit and a credit entry. This ensures that the accounting equation remains balanced:

Assets = Liabilities + Owner‚Äôs Equity

Every account in accounting falls under a specific classification and has a normal balance (debit or credit). Understanding these classifications is essential for proper recording.

Account Classifications and Their Normal Balances

  • 1. Asset Accounts ‚Äì properties or resources owned by the business.
    Normal Balance: Debit

    Normal Balance: Credit
  • 3. Owner‚Äôs Equity Accounts ‚Äì owner‚Äôs investment and claims on assets.
    Normal Balance: Credit
  • 4. Revenue Accounts ‚Äì income earned from providing goods or services.
    Normal Balance: Credit
  • 5. Expense Accounts ‚Äì costs incurred to run the business.
    Normal Balance: Debit
