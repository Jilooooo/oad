Introduction to Risk Management

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and prioritizing risks, followed by implementing strategies to minimize, monitor, and control the likelihood or impact of negative events.

Safety and Security

Safety refers to the protection of people from harm.

Security refers to the protection of property from damage or loss.

Event organizers must take responsibility for protecting:

  • People
  • Property
  • Reputation
  • Revenues

Areas that must be evaluated for safety, security, and risks include:

  • Design of the venue and event site
  • Stage and other structures
  • Special effects
  • Electrical connections
  • Crowd control
  • Transportation
  • Sanitation facilities and waste management

Risk Assessment

Hazard ‚Äì something that has the potential to cause harm or injury to people, or loss or damage to property.

Risk ‚Äì the probability that the hazard will cause harm, taking into account exposure and context.

The 5-Step Approach to Risk Assessment

1. Identify the Hazard

Determine what could cause harm and who might be affected. Example: An electrical short circuit can harm a person nearby and may start a fire.

2. Assess the Risk

Evaluate the likelihood and severity of the hazard‚Äôs impact.

3. Develop Controls

Reduce or eliminate the risks by:

  • Eliminating the hazard at the source
  • Substituting with safer equipment or methods
  • Reducing the risk and measuring the reduction
  • Isolating people and objects from the hazard

4. Implement Controls

Apply the most practical and effective methods to reduce risks before the event.

5. Monitor and Review

Continuously check for new risks before, during, and after the event. Take action immediately when new hazards are identified.

Contingency Planning

Contingency planning is a key component of risk management. It involves creating backup plans or strategies to be implemented if identified risks occur.

These plans ensure that the organization or event can continue functioning or quickly recover from unexpected problems or emergencies.

Summary

Risk management and contingency planning ensure the safety and success of an event. By identifying hazards, assessing risks, implementing controls, and preparing backup plans, organizers can protect people, property, reputation, and revenue. Effective contingency planning allows for quick recovery from unforeseen problems, ensuring the event continues smoothly and safely.
