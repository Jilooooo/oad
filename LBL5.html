What is Agency?

Agency is a contract where one person, called the agent, acts on behalf of another, called the principal.

  • The agent represents the principal in business transactions.
  • The principal is bound by acts of the agent done within authority.
  • Agency is based on trust and confidence.

Obligations of the Agent and Principal

  • Agent must:
    • Follow the instructions of the principal.
    • Render accounting of transactions.
    • Act with diligence and good faith.
    • Not exceed authority.
  • Principal must:
    • Pay the agent agreed compensation.
    • Reimburse expenses lawfully incurred.
    • Respect obligations entered into by the agent within authority.

What is Partnership?

Partnership is a contract where two or more persons bind themselves to contribute money, property, or industry to a common fund with the intention of dividing profits among themselves.

  • Partnership requires mutual contribution.
  • Partners share profits and losses.
  • Partnership has a separate juridical personality.

Types of Partners

  • General Partner ‚Äì liable up to personal property for obligations.
  • Limited Partner ‚Äì liability limited to their contribution.
  • Capitalist Partner ‚Äì contributes money or property.
  • Industrial Partner ‚Äì contributes labor or industry.

Formation and Dissolution of Partnership

  • Partnership is formed when:
    • There is a valid agreement between partners.
    • Each contributes to the common fund.
    • The partnership is for a lawful purpose.
  • Partnership is dissolved when:
    • A partner withdraws or dies.
    • The partnership term expires.
    • The business becomes unlawful.
    • Partners mutually agree to dissolve.

Agency vs. Partnership

  • Agency: One acts on behalf of another; no sharing of profits.
  • Partnership: Two or more persons operate a business together and share profits.
  • Agency is based on representation, while partnership is based on association.

Importance in Business

  • Agency allows companies to delegate duties through representatives.
  • Partnerships allow pooling of resources for larger business ventures.
  • Both create legally recognized business relationships.
  • They define rights to avoid conflicts and liabilities.
