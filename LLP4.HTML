Lesson 4 – Legal Correspondence and Communication

Learning Objectives

  • Explain what legal correspondence is and its purpose in a legal office.
  • Identify the different types of legal correspondence.
  • Describe the proper tone and language used in legal communication.
  • Recognize the importance of professionalism in telephone and email etiquette.

Introduction

Legal correspondence refers to all written and electronic communication used in a legal office. It includes letters, emails, memoranda, and reports exchanged between lawyers, staff, courts, clients, and government offices.

What is Legal Correspondence?

  • Keeps records of case updates and instructions
  • Ensures professionalism and accuracy
  • Communicates clearly with clients, courts, and agencies
  • Helps maintain confidentiality and proper documentation

Types of Legal Correspondence

1. Letters to Clients

  • Contain updates or reminders
  • Example: Notifying a hearing date

2. Letters to Lawyers

Example: Settlement proposal

3. Letters to Courts

Formal letters accompanying pleadings

4. Letters to Government Offices

Request documents or certifications

5. Transmittal Letters

Example: “Enclosed is the notarized affidavit.”

6. Demand Letters

Example: “Please settle within 10 days.”

Use of Legal Language and Tone

  • Formal and respectful
  • No slang or emotional wording
  • Concise and factual

Office Memoranda and Reports

1. Office Memoranda

Example: Reminder to update client files

2. Reports

Updates on case progress or research

Telephone & Email Etiquette

Telephone

  • Answer within three rings
  • Use professional greetings
  • Maintain confidentiality

Email

  • Formal subject lines and greetings
  • Proofread
  • Professional signature

What is legal correspondence?

  • Informal chat
  • Formal written communication
  • Court hearings
  • Verbal orders

Who receives letters about hearing dates?

  • Clients
  • Messenger
  • Judge
  • None

Summary

Legal correspondence ensures clear and professional communication between lawyers, clients, courts, and agencies.
