Lesson 2 – Legal Terminologies and Documents

Introduction

Legal offices use many specialized terms and documents in preparing cases, filing paperwork, and communicating with clients and the court. Understanding these terms is essential for anyone working in a law office, as they ensure accuracy, professionalism, and proper handling of legal tasks.

Common Legal Terms

These terms are frequently used in legal documents, court procedures, and law office operations. Knowing them helps legal office staff manage documents and communicate correctly.

  • Affidavit – A written statement of facts sworn before a notary public.
  • Complaint – A legal document filed by a plaintiff to begin a lawsuit.
  • Defendant – The person or party being sued or accused in a case.
  • Plaintiff – The person or party who files the complaint.
  • Subpoena – A court order requiring a person to appear or present documents.
  • Deposition – A sworn testimony given outside the courtroom.
  • Summons – A notice informing the defendant that a case was filed.
  • Verdict – The formal decision or judgment of a court.
  • Notary Public – A person authorized to notarize documents and administer oaths.
  • Testimony – A statement given under oath during court proceedings.

Types of Legal Documents

1. Pleadings

Formal written statements submitted to the court by the parties involved.

  • Complaint – Filed by the plaintiff to begin the case.
  • Answer – Defendant’s response to the complaint.
  • Reply – Plaintiff's response to the defendant’s answer.

2. Contracts and Agreements

Legal documents stating mutual obligations between parties.

  • Employment Contract
  • Lease Agreement
  • Sales Contract
  • Service Agreement

3. Affidavits and Notarized Documents

Sworn written statements signed before a notary public.

  • Affidavit of Loss
  • Affidavit of Support
  • Affidavit of Undertaking

4. Memoranda and Legal Opinions

Internal or advisory legal documents prepared by lawyers or legal staff.

  • Memorandum of Law – Explains legal arguments or case analysis.
  • Legal Opinion – Provides legal interpretation or advice.

Formatting and Preparing Legal Papers

Legal documents must follow strict formatting rules to ensure professionalism and compliance with court requirements. Incorrect formatting may result in delays or rejection of filings.

  • Paper Size: Long bond (8.5 x 13 inches).
  • Margins: Left – 1.5 inches; Right/Top/Bottom – 1 inch.
  • Font Style & Size: Times New Roman or Arial, size 12.
  • Spacing: Double-spaced for pleadings; single-spaced for internal documents.
  • Headings: Must include court name, case number, and document title.
  • Pagination: Page number at bottom center or bottom right.

What is an Affidavit?

  • A court verdict
  • A sworn written statement notarized by a notary public
  • A legal complaint
  • A verbal testimony

Which document begins a lawsuit?

  • Complaint
  • Verdict
  • Subpoena
  • Affidavit

A Summons is used to:

  • End a trial
  • Notify a defendant about a filed case
  • Record a testimony
  • Approve a contract

Which is NOT a type of pleading?

  • Answer
  • Lease Agreement
  • Reply

Summary

Lesson 2 introduced essential legal terms and the main types of legal documents handled in a legal office. Mastery of these concepts helps ensure accuracy, professionalism, and proper preparation of case files. Understanding formatting rules is also important to comply with court standards and avoid filing errors.
